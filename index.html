What do the bars above indicate?

Guessing Difficulty: Measures how resistant your password is to brute-force guessing. Longer passwords generally take more time to crack, so this score improves with length.
Uncommonness: Evaluates how diverse your character selection is. Using a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols makes it harder to predict.
Cracking Difficulty: Based on entropy — a measure of unpredictability. The higher the entropy, the stronger the resistance to dictionary or rainbow table attacks.
Overall Strength: A combined evaluation of the above, giving you a simple snapshot of your password’s real-world strength.
